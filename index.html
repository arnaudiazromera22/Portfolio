About Me: My Journey

Truth be told, I got into computing because I wanted to create my own video games. But as soon as I started studying, I discovered that the command line and cybersecurity hooked me much more than development. I'm not saying I won't program a game someday, but right now my focus has completely changed.

Even before starting my degree, I was always curious to know how computers worked on the inside and looked for ways to learn on my own.

I am currently 18 years old and studying 1st year of ASIX (Administration of Network Computer Systems). If I told you I have years of experience, I would be lying: I have just started. What I do have is a very fast learning capacity and a lot of desire to get my hands dirty with real projects.

What drives me

What I like most about systems administration is that you never get bored. There is always a log to check, a service to configure, or a network to secure.

As I always say, it's not enough for something to just "work". I need to understand why it worked or why it failed.

My goal is to finish the degree knowing how to set up solid and secure infrastructures. I am motivated by technical challenges, especially those that make others give up.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is your main background and experience?

I have completed the Intermediate Degree in Microcomputer Systems and Networks (SMX), where I learned the basics of equipment assembly and technical support. Now, at 18, I am in 1st year of ASIX to delve deeper into server administration and complex networks. My real experience comes from my SMX internship and the projects I set up on my own at home.

What technologies do you use in your projects?

On a daily basis, what I touch most is Linux (Ubuntu/Debian) and Windows Server. For network labs I use Cisco Packet Tracer, and to set up my test environments I use VirtualBox or VMware. I also like to automate simple tasks using scripts in Bash.

How do you tackle technical challenges when something fails?

I'm quite curious and, above all, stubborn. If something doesn't work or a service doesn't run, I don't give up. I like to check the logs, investigate the error in the documentation, and not stop until I find the root cause. For me, making something work again is fine, but understanding why it broke is what really teaches me.

How can I contact you for a collaboration or interview?

The easiest way is to contact me via LinkedIn or send me an email (links below). I am very motivated to find opportunities where I can apply what I know and, above all, keep learning from people with more experience in the systems and cybersecurity sector.
