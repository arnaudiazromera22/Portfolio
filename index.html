About Me

My Journey So Far

I actually got into IT because I wanted to create my own video game. But as I started studying, I realized that the command line and everything related to cybersecurity actually hooked me even more than game dev. I’m not saying "no" to making a game one day, but my focus has definitely shifted.

Even before I started my formal studies, I always had questions about how computers worked and I was constantly looking for ways to expand my knowledge.

Right now, I’m in my first year of ASIX (Network Systems Administration). If I told you I have years of work experience, I’d be lying—I’m just getting started. However, what I do have is the ability to learn very fast and a genuine excitement for picking up new skills every day.

What drives me?

What I love most about systems administration is that you never get bored. There’s always a log to check, a service that needs tweaking, or a network that needs securing.

Like I mentioned earlier, just getting things to work isn't enough for me—I need to understand why something happened in the first place.

My goal is to finish my degree knowing how to build solid and, above all, secure infrastructures. I’m driven by the challenge of finding the root cause of a problem when everyone else is ready to give up.
